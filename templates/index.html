Voluntron's Story

Voluntron is an innovative website developed by a group of motivated high schoolers dedicated to fostering connections and supporting communities, in Canton and around the United States. Our dynamic platform serves as a hub, linking passionate volunteers with individuals seeking help.

Welcome to commUNITY

Refugees face numerous hardships when they arrive in a new country, and the situation is no different for those who come to the United States. They often have to navigate complex legal systems, learn a new language, and adapt to a different culture while dealing with the trauma of leaving their homes behind. In Michigan, many refugees also struggle to find affordable housing, healthcare, and education.

Despite these challenges, refugees are often some of the most resilient and resourceful members of our communities. By providing them with the support and resources they need to succeed, we can help them to rebuild their lives and contribute to the greater good.

At commUNITY, we are deeply committed to helping refugees in need. Our organization is based in Ann Arbor, Michigan and operates out of the MCA masjid. We take a unique approach to refugee aid by collecting real-world data to determine the most pressing needs of refugees in our community. This allows us to better pair available supplies with those who need them the most.

If you are interested in joining us in our mission to support refugees, we encourage you to visit our HELP US page to learn more about how you can get involved.

As Muslims, we are called upon to be charitable and to help those in need. This is reflected in numerous hadiths, or sayings of the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him), that emphasize the importance of giving to others.

One such hadith states: “Protect yourself from hell-fire even by giving a piece of date as charity.” (Al-Bukhari and Muslim) This hadith reminds us that even the smallest acts of charity can have a significant impact and help us to earn Allah's reward and protection.

Another hadith tells us that: “The believer’s shade on the Day of Resurrection will be his charity.” (Al-Tirmidhi) This beautiful hadith emphasizes the importance of giving generously to others, as it will be a source of comfort and shade for us on the Day of Judgment.

As Muslims, it is our duty to care for those in need and to give generously to charity. We hope that you will join us in our efforts to support refugees and make a positive difference in the lives of those who are struggling.
